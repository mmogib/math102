\mbox{}

\vspace{1mm}
The geometric series
\[
\displaystyle
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}ar^{n-1}=a+ar+ar^2+\cdots \text{ converges if } |r|<1 \text{ and its sum is} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}ar^{n-1}=\frac{a}{1-r}, |r|<1
\]
if $|r|\geq 1$, the geometric series is divergent.

\vspace{3mm}
\hrule
\vspace{2mm}
{\bf Divergence Test} If $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}a_n$ does not exist or if $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}a_n\not=0$, then the series $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n$ is divergent.
\vspace{3mm}
\hrule
\vspace{2mm}
The harmonic series $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n}$ is divergent.
\end{minipage}
